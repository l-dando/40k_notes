## 🧭 Overview
*The Reflection Crack’d* explores the [[Emperor]]’s Children in the aftermath of the [[Drop Site Massacre]], charting their descent into Chaos and the growing instability of their Primarch, [[Fulgrim]]. Ordered by [[Horus Lupercal]] to proceed to Mars, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Fulgrim|Fulgrim]] instead diverts his Legion to assault a Mechanicus resource world, pursuing his own designs. When questioned, he slays Lord Commander [[Eidolon]] outright, shocking his sons and further exposing his erratic behaviour.

[[Lucius]], increasingly suspicious of his Primarch, begins to study him closely. Disturbed by [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Fulgrim|Fulgrim]]’s mood swings, lack of camaraderie, and declining swordsmanship, [[Lucius]] concludes that [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Fulgrim|Fulgrim]] is not who he appears to be after witnessing him wield sorcerous powers. Guided by dark dreams, he investigates La Fenice aboard the *Pride of the [[Emperor]]*, discovering a portrait of [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Fulgrim|Fulgrim]] that seems to contain his trapped spirit. Convinced that a daemon has taken his master’s place, [[Lucius]] convenes the Brotherhood of the Phoenix. Though despised by his peers, he gains allies in [[Julius Kaesoron]] and [[Fabius Bile]], who reveal their own suspicions and evidence of [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Fulgrim|Fulgrim]]’s transformation. Together with [[Marius Vairosean]], they ambush and capture their Primarch.

In the Apothecarion, the conspirators torture [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Fulgrim|Fulgrim]] in an attempt to drive out the possessing entity. Throughout, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Fulgrim|Fulgrim]] speaks calmly of his vision for the Legion, until [[Lucius]] realises he has misjudged the situation. [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Fulgrim|Fulgrim]] frees himself effortlessly, revealing the truth: he had indeed been possessed, his spirit trapped in the painting, but he mastered the daemon and reversed their places, imprisoning it instead. The dreams that guided [[Lucius]] were the daemon’s attempt to escape. [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Fulgrim|Fulgrim]] explains that his apparent weaknesses were deliberate ploys to educate [[Lucius]] in Chaos’s power, and that his erratic nature is simply the evolution of his being. Declaring his intent to push experience and sensation to their extremes, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Fulgrim|Fulgrim]] announces the next step in his journey — to join forces with [[Perturabo]] and the Iron Warriors.
