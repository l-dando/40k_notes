## 🧭 Overview

The 63rd Expeditionary Fleet, led by Warmaster [[Horus Lupercal]], arrives on [[Heresy_Omnibus/Places/Davin|Davin]] to meet elements of the Word Bearers under First Chaplain [[Erebus]]. Among the arrivals is [[Petronella Vivar]], a noblewoman from Terra assigned to chronicle Horus’s deeds. At a War Council, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Erebus|Erebus]] claims that planetary governor [[Eugen Temba]] has turned traitor after leading his forces to [[Heresy_Omnibus/Places/Davin|Davin]]’s moon to suppress a tribal rebellion. Horus, enraged, orders an immediate assault.

[[Ignace Karkasy]] reports the meeting to [[Garviel Loken]], suggesting [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Erebus|Erebus]] manipulated Horus. Their conversation is interrupted by [[Ezekyle Abaddon]], who defends [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Erebus|Erebus]] and scorns Loken’s association with remembrancers. When Karkasy questions Abaddon about a silver coin he gave [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Erebus|Erebus]], Abaddon tries to kill him, but Loken intervenes and swears Karkasy to silence.

The “speartip” — six companies of the Sons of Horus, the Byzant Janizars, three Titans of the Legio Mortis, and [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Erebus|Erebus]]’s Word Bearers — lands on [[Heresy_Omnibus/Places/Davin|Davin]]’s moon. Instead of dry forest, they find a stinking, rotted swamp. A broadcast praising “Nurgh‑leth” echoes eerily, reminding Loken of the Whisperheads.

---

Advancing through the mire, the Sons of Horus are attacked by plague‑ridden corpses of Temba’s men. The Titan *Dies Irae*, blinded by fog, shoots down an incoming craft — [[Petronella Vivar]]’s skiff, defying Horus’s orders. Her bodyguard [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Maggard|Maggard]] holds off the undead until the burning wreck draws Astartes to their position.

The force discovers the wreck of Temba’s flagship, the *Glory of Terra*, rotting and broadcasting the unsettling signal. Horus leads four companies inside, leaving Loken and [[Tarik Torgaddon]] to guard the entrance. Inside, Horus is separated from his men and fights through plague zombies to reach the bridge, where he confronts a bloated, corrupted Temba wielding the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Items/Anathame|Anathame]]. Horus is wounded in the shoulder before killing Temba, who dies repentant, confessing his fall to Chaos. Destroying the broadcast machinery ends the zombie threat just in time to save the outer defence.

Horus collapses from his wound. The [[Heresy_Omnibus/Items/Mournival|Mournival]] rush him back to the fleet, but the hangar is choked with panicked civilians. In their desperation, Abaddon and others kill or maim those in their way. From above, Karkasy and [[Mersadie Oliton]] watch in horror as [[Euphrati Keeler]] distributes *Lectitio Divinitatus* pamphlets to the wounded. Apothecaries keep Horus alive but cannot halt the toxin’s spread. Believing he is dying, Horus confides in Vivar, revealing secrets of the primarch brotherhood.

Seeking answers, Loken and Torgaddon return to the *Glory of Terra* with [[Tybalt Marr]], finding the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Items/Anathame|Anathame]]. Loken recognises it as the blade stolen from the Hall of Devices on Xenobia, concluding [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Erebus|Erebus]] orchestrated Horus’s wounding. Meanwhile, the Sons of Horus’ warrior lodge, swayed by [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Erebus|Erebus]], decides to take Horus to the Serpent Lodge temple on [[Heresy_Omnibus/Places/Davin|Davin]], claiming its occult rites can heal him.

---

At the temple, the lodge seals Horus inside. In the spirit realm, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Erebus|Erebus]] — disguised as [[Hastur Sejanus]] — guides Horus through visions: a shrine‑world of the far future where the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]] and nine loyal primarchs are worshipped as gods, secret laboratories beneath Terra where the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]] used Warp‑born knowledge to create the primarchs, and the scattering of the primarchs. [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Erebus|Erebus]] claims the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]] seeks godhood for himself and will abandon his sons. Horus also glimpses a hellish industrial wasteland beneath the idyllic surface.

In the material realm, Loken learns from [[Kyril Sindermann]] of the link between serpent cults and Chaos. Karkasy writes damning poetry about the embarkation deck massacre. Keeler, attacked for her faith, is rescued by Torgaddon and taken to Sindermann. Translating the *Book of Lorgar*, they accidentally summon a daemon, which Keeler miraculously banishes by channelling the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]]’s power before collapsing into a coma.

In the Warp, [[Magnus the Red]] appears as the leader of the wolf pack in Horus’s vision, stripping [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Erebus|Erebus]] of his disguise and warning Horus of manipulation. Distrustful of Magnus yet inflamed by pride and betrayal, Horus resolves to rebel against the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]]. He emerges from the temple healed, to the cheers of his Legion.

---

The 63rd Expedition moves on, encountering the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Places/Auretian Technocracy|Auretian Technocracy]] — an advanced human civilisation. Horus engineers war by slaughtering their ambassadors, promising their STCs to Adept [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Regulus|Regulus]] of the Mechanicum in exchange for support. The lodge plots to hand Loken over for execution to appease [[Hektor Varvarus]] and silence Karkasy, but Torgaddon refuses and quits the lodge.

Joined by [[Angron]] and the World Eaters, the war lasts nine brutal months. During a visit from the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]]’s Children, [[Fabius Bile]] takes the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Items/Anathame|Anathame]] and gives it to [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Fulgrim|Fulgrim]]. In the final assault, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Angron|Angron]] is buried in rubble; as the last Auretians surrender to Varvarus, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Angron|Angron]] bursts free and attacks, forcing a massacre in which Varvarus is killed.

After Varvarus’s funeral, Horus begins “cleaning house.” [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Maggard|Maggard]], now a lodge member, murders Karkasy; his death is passed off as suicide. Horus kills [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Petronella Vivar|Petronella Vivar]] himself to silence her. Loken and Torgaddon vow to resist the Legion’s corruption. In a secret meeting, Horus declares his intent to overthrow the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]] and begins planning an operation in the Isstvan system.

- Chaos as both ideology and metaphysical force

## 📝 Notes
- [[Horus Lupercal]]’s wound on [[Heresy_Omnibus/Places/Davin|Davin]] is both literal and symbolic.  
- The Serpent Lodge masks corruption with the guise of healing.  
- [[Garviel Loken]]’s outsider view remains the reader’s moral compass.
