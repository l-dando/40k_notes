# 🧭 Overview

---

## Book One — Terra Figula
[[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Perturabo|Perturabo]]’s earliest memory is of climbing a cliff on Olympia, foreshadowing a lifetime of struggle and defiance. In the present, the Iron Warriors besiege the Cadmean Citadel on Hydra Cordatus. After months of grinding warfare, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Perturabo|Perturabo]] personally intervenes to break the Imperial Fists’ defence, promoting the brutal [[Kroeger]] and demoting the reckless [[Harkor]].

The [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]]’s Children arrive unexpectedly, shocking the Iron Warriors with their mutated decadence. Fulgrim introduces an Aeldari guide, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Karuchi Vohra|Karuchi Vohra]], and demands [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Perturabo|Perturabo]] construct the Thaliakron so he can recount the legend of the Angel Exterminatus — a weapon of unimaginable power. Hidden Shattered Legion operatives, [[Sabik Wayland]] and [[Nykona Sharrowkyn]], attempt to assassinate Fulgrim, but he survives.

Fulgrim reveals his true goal: to enter a warp rift and claim the Angel Exterminatus. [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Perturabo|Perturabo]] reluctantly agrees, warning the Trident ([[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Forrix|Forrix]], Falk, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Kroeger|Kroeger]]) not to trust the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]]’s Children. Meanwhile, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Fabius Bile|Fabius Bile]] experiments on captured Imperial Fists, and Fulgrim secretly plots to resurrect the Angel Exterminatus for himself.

---

## Book Two — As Above, So Below
On Medusa IV, a vision of [[Ferrus Manus]] hunts a wyrm, symbolising the lingering trauma of Fulgrim’s fratricide.

Fulgrim, haunted by memories, reconvenes the Brotherhood of the Phoenix. On the Sisypheum, the Shattered Legions prepare to oppose the traitors. [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Cassander|Cassander]] attempts to kill Fabius but is transformed into a terata monstrosity. The loyalists navigate the Paths Below while the traitors approach the warp rift — now named the **Eye of Terror** by [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Perturabo|Perturabo]].

Tensions rise within both Legions. [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Eidolon|Eidolon]] attempts to corrupt [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Vull Bronn|Vull Bronn]], [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Grendel|Grendel]] intervenes, and [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Thamatica|Thamatica]]’s experiments expose the Sisypheum’s location. [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Perturabo|Perturabo]] boards the loyalist ship, slaughtering defenders while Fabius raids the apothecarion. A catastrophic energy spike forces the Iron Warriors to evacuate their Primarch. [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Thamatica|Thamatica]] overcharges the Sisypheum’s reactors, sending it crashing through the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]]’s Children flagship. [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Perturabo|Perturabo]] allows the loyalists to escape as punishment for Fulgrim’s deceit.

[[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Perturabo|Perturabo]] confronts Fulgrim, asserting command of the expedition.

---

## Book Three — Coniunctio
On Chemos, Fulgrim’s origin is retold: a miraculous child found in a polluted wasteland, destined for greatness.

The traitors reach the Crone World Iydris, whose very landscape resists them. As they advance through the haunted sepulchre, the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]]’s Children succumb to sensory madness while Aeldari spirits rise to defend their dead world. Shattered Legion survivors infiltrate the complex from another route.

Fulgrim reveals his true plan: to sacrifice [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Perturabo|Perturabo]] and ascend as the Angel Exterminatus. A gemstone gifted to [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Perturabo|Perturabo]] secretly drains his life. The Iron Warriors fracture under internal betrayal as [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Toramino|Toramino]] shells [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Forrix|Forrix]]’s position, and [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Kroeger|Kroeger]] kills [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Harkor|Harkor]] in a frenzy.

Fulgrim rises through the sepulchre’s core, leaving [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Perturabo|Perturabo]] dying. [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Eidolon|Eidolon]] begins the ritual sacrifice — but [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Perturabo|Perturabo]] seizes the gemstone and confronts Fulgrim in a vision of an Olympia that never was. He hurls the artifact away, only for [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Branthan|Branthan]]’s psyber‑eagle to snatch it.

The loyalists strike: Numen kills [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Marius Vairosean|Marius Vairosean]], [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Grendel|Grendel]] destroys Bombastus, and Sharrowkyn slays [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Lucius|Lucius]]. The gemstone is shattered, freeing [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Perturabo|Perturabo]] to strike Fulgrim. The Primarch of the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]]’s Children splits open and ascends as a Daemon Prince of Slaanesh, whisking his Legion away.

As Iydris collapses, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Perturabo|Perturabo]] leads his sons to safety and orders the fleet into the black hole consuming the Crone World. [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Lucius|Lucius]], resurrected by Slaanesh, awakens amid Fabius’s laboratory as the terata burn, leaving only one gene‑sample intact.

## 🔗 Connections
- Chronicles Fulgrim’s full transformation into a Daemon Prince.
- Deepens [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Perturabo|Perturabo]]’s character, his rivalry with Fulgrim, and his bitterness toward the Imperium.
- Features major Shattered Legion figures: Sharrowkyn, Wayland, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Branthan|Branthan]], Tarsa.
- Introduces the Eye of Terror’s naming.
- Advances the Iron Warriors’ internal politics: [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Kroeger|Kroeger]], [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Forrix|Forrix]], Falk, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Harkor|Harkor]], [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Toramino|Toramino]].
- Explores the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]]’s Children’s descent into excess and mutation.
