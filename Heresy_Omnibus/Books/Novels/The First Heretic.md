## 🧭 Overview
*The First Heretic* chronicles the fall of the Word Bearers — the first Legion to turn from the [[Emperor]] — and follows their Primarch, [[Lorgar Aurelian|Lorgar]], and Captain [[Argel Tal]] through the events that ignite the [[Horus Lupercal|Horus]] Heresy. It is a story of faith, betrayal, revelation, and damnation, tracing the XVII Legion’s transformation from devout Imperial missionaries into the Imperium’s most fanatical heretics.

---

## Part One — Grey
In 962.M30, the perfect city of [[Monarchia]], built by the Word Bearers in worship of the [[Emperor]], is forcibly evacuated and annihilated by the Ultramarines. Among the survivors is [[Cyrene Valantion]], blinded by the bombardment. When the Word Bearers arrive, [[Lorgar Aurelian|Lorgar]] confronts [[Malcador the Sigillite]], only for the [[Emperor]] Himself to descend and condemn [[Lorgar Aurelian|Lorgar]] for worshipping Him as a god. Before the entire Legion, the [[Emperor]] forces them to kneel in the dust.

Shattered by this humiliation, [[Lorgar Aurelian|Lorgar]] turns to [[Kor Phaeron]] and [[Erebus]], who tell him the [[Emperor]] is a flawed god — and that perhaps the old faith of Colchis held truths he abandoned too quickly. Desperate for meaning, [[Lorgar Aurelian|Lorgar]] agrees to undertake the **Pilgrimage**, an ancient quest to seek the realm where gods and mortals meet.

Returning to Colchis, [[Lorgar Aurelian|Lorgar]] speaks with [[Magnus the Red]], who refuses to answer his questions about gods in the Warp. [[Lorgar Aurelian|Lorgar]] appoints [[Cyrene Valantion|Cyrene]] as a **Confessor** of the Legion and joins the **1,301st Expedition Fleet**, commanded by [[Argel Tal]]’s Serrated Sun Chapter, to begin the Pilgrimage beyond Imperial space.

---

## Part Two — Pilgrimage
Three years later, the expedition reaches a region where the Warp bleeds into reality. A psychic scream calls [[Lorgar Aurelian|Lorgar]]’s name from a world on the storm’s edge — [[Cadia]]. There, the Word Bearers encounter tribes who speak Colchisian, wear human‑skin pelts, and worship gods of the Warp. Their leader, [[Ingethel]], welcomes them as prophesied visitors.

During a ritual, [[Lorgar Aurelian|Lorgar]] is told he must choose a tenth sacrifice. When the Custodian [[Vendatha]] intervenes, violence erupts. [[Lorgar Aurelian|Lorgar]] orders [[Vendatha]] impaled, completing the ritual. [[Ingethel]] transforms into a daemon — **[[Ingethel]] the Ascended** — and offers to reveal the truth of the universe, but demands a price: some of [[Lorgar Aurelian|Lorgar]]’s sons must be offered first. [[Lorgar Aurelian|Lorgar]] chooses **[[Argel Tal]] and the Seventh Assault Company**.

Aboard the *Orfeo’s Lament*, [[Ingethel]] shows the company visions: the Fall of the Eldar, the [[Emperor]]’s secret primarch project beneath Terra, and the revelation that the [[Emperor]] bargained with the gods of the Warp to create His sons. Betrayed to their core, the Word Bearers turn against the [[Emperor]] in their hearts.

[[Ingethel]] then demands they lower the Geller field. When they obey, she slaughters them — only for their corpses to rise again, possessed by daemons. The Seventh Company endures a seven‑month nightmare of cannibalism, blood‑drinking, and madness before escaping the storm. For the rest of the fleet, only seconds have passed.

[[Lorgar Aurelian|Lorgar]] senses the daemons within them and records [[Argel Tal]]’s testimony. He embraces the Primordial Truth, exterminates the Cadian tribes to hide their tracks, and elevates the survivors as the **Gal Vorbak**, the first possessed Astartes. In secret, he orders them to hide their nature until the time comes to rebel.

---

## Part Three — Crimson
Forty years later, [[Argel Tal]] — now *the Crimson Lord* — commands the 1,301st Expedition. The Gal Vorbak fight alongside [[Aquillon]] and the Custodians, their daemons stirring ominously. Then news arrives: **[[Horus Lupercal|Horus]] has turned against the [[Emperor]]**.

The Word Bearers join the Iron Warriors, Night Lords, and Alpha Legion at [[Isstvan V]]. As the [[Drop Site Massacre]] unfolds, [[Argel Tal]]’s daemon [[Raum]] fully awakens, whispering prophecies of their fate. When the [[Raven Guard]] retreat toward the Word Bearers’ lines, [[Argel Tal]] silently asks his long‑dead family for forgiveness before giving the order to fire.

The Gal Vorbak unleash their daemonic forms, becoming monstrous avatars of slaughter. [[Corvus Corax|Corax]] kills half their number before duelling [[Lorgar Aurelian|Lorgar]] in a cataclysmic clash. [[Argel Tal]]’s full daemonic potential erupts, but [[Lorgar Aurelian|Lorgar]] is nearly slain until [[Konrad Curze]] intervenes. [[Dagotal]] dies in flames, his death felt by [[Argel Tal]] like a wound.

After the massacre, [[Aquillon]] and his Custodians arrive aboard the *De Profundis* and uncover the Word Bearers’ treachery. They confront [[Cyrene Valantion|Cyrene]], killing Incarnadine and then murdering her moments before [[Argel Tal]] arrives. She dies in his arms.

Consumed by grief and rage, [[Argel Tal]] hunts the Custodians to the surface of [[Isstvan V]]. In the final confrontation, [[Malnor]] is butchered, [[Aquillon]] is decapitated by [[Argel Tal]], and the last Custodian kills [[Xaphen]] before being torn apart by the surviving Gal Vorbak.

Alone with [[Cyrene Valantion|Cyrene]]’s final message, [[Argel Tal]] mourns his fallen brothers as the Word Bearers set course for **Calth** to ambush the Ultramarines. In the silence of his mind, [[Raum]] speaks again — declaring that he is [[Argel Tal]]’s brother now.

## 🧠 Themes
- Faith supplanting duty  
- The birth of heresy through ritual  
- Betrayal as spiritual revelation  

## ⚔️ Legion Focus
- Word Bearers: [[Lorgar Aurelian]], [[Erebus]], [[Kor Phaeron]]  

## 📝 Notes
- The First Heretic reframes loyalty as worship, showing how zealotry can outstrip even genetics.  
- Dark Apostles serve as the ideological backbone for the [[Drop Site Massacre]].  
- [[Cadia]]’s early exposure to Chaos foreshadows its later role as the Imperium’s bulwark.

## 🔗 Connections
- Builds on the censure event on [[Monarchia]] from earlier volumes.  
- Foreshadows the [[Drop Site Massacre]] at [[Isstvan V]].  
- Sets the stage for *[[Galaxy in Flames]]* and the wider [[Horus Lupercal|Horus]] Heresy series.
