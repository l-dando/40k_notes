## 🧭 Overview
*The First Heretic* chronicles the fall of the Word Bearers — the first Legion to turn from the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]] — and follows their Primarch, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Lorgar Aurelian|Lorgar]], and Captain [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Argel Tal|Argel Tal]] through the events that ignite the Horus Heresy. It is a story of faith, betrayal, revelation, and damnation, tracing the XVII Legion’s transformation from devout Imperial missionaries into the Imperium’s most fanatical heretics.

---

## Part One — Grey
In 962.M30, the perfect city of **[[Heresy_Omnibus/Places/Monarchia|Monarchia]]**, built by the Word Bearers in worship of the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]], is forcibly evacuated and annihilated by the Ultramarines. Among the survivors is **[[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Cyrene Valantion|Cyrene Valantion]]**, blinded by the bombardment. When the Word Bearers arrive, Lorgar confronts **[[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Malcador the Sigillite|Malcador the Sigillite]]**, only for the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]] Himself to descend and condemn Lorgar for worshipping Him as a god. Before the entire Legion, the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]] forces them to kneel in the dust.

Shattered by this humiliation, Lorgar turns to **[[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Kor Phaeron|Kor Phaeron]]** and **[[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Erebus|Erebus]]**, who tell him the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]] is a flawed god — and that perhaps the old faith of Colchis held truths he abandoned too quickly. Desperate for meaning, Lorgar agrees to undertake the **Pilgrimage**, an ancient quest to seek the realm where gods and mortals meet.

Returning to Colchis, Lorgar speaks with **[[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Magnus the Red|Magnus the Red]]**, who refuses to answer his questions about gods in the Warp. Lorgar appoints Cyrene as a **Confessor** of the Legion and joins the **1,301st Expedition Fleet**, commanded by [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Argel Tal|Argel Tal]]’s Serrated Sun Chapter, to begin the Pilgrimage beyond Imperial space.

---

## Part Two — Pilgrimage
Three years later, the expedition reaches a region where the Warp bleeds into reality. A psychic scream calls Lorgar’s name from a world on the storm’s edge — **[[Heresy_Omnibus/Places/Cadia|Cadia]]**. There, the Word Bearers encounter tribes who speak Colchisian, wear human‑skin pelts, and worship gods of the Warp. Their leader, **[[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Ingethel|Ingethel]]**, welcomes them as prophesied visitors.

During a ritual, Lorgar is told he must choose a tenth sacrifice. When the Custodian **[[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Vendatha|Vendatha]]** intervenes, violence erupts. Lorgar orders [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Vendatha|Vendatha]] impaled, completing the ritual. [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Ingethel|Ingethel]] transforms into a daemon — **[[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Ingethel|Ingethel]] the Ascended** — and offers to reveal the truth of the universe, but demands a price: some of Lorgar’s sons must be offered first. Lorgar chooses **[[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Argel Tal|Argel Tal]] and the Seventh Assault Company**.

Aboard the *Orfeo’s Lament*, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Ingethel|Ingethel]] shows the company visions: the Fall of the Eldar, the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]]’s secret primarch project beneath Terra, and the revelation that the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]] bargained with the gods of the Warp to create His sons. Betrayed to their core, the Word Bearers turn against the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]] in their hearts.

[[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Ingethel|Ingethel]] then demands they lower the Geller field. When they obey, she slaughters them — only for their corpses to rise again, possessed by daemons. The Seventh Company endures a seven‑month nightmare of cannibalism, blood‑drinking, and madness before escaping the storm. For the rest of the fleet, only seconds have passed.

Lorgar senses the daemons within them and records [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Argel Tal|Argel Tal]]’s testimony. He embraces the Primordial Truth, exterminates the Cadian tribes to hide their tracks, and elevates the survivors as the **Gal Vorbak**, the first possessed Astartes. In secret, he orders them to hide their nature until the time comes to rebel.

---

## Part Three — Crimson
Forty years later, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Argel Tal|Argel Tal]] — now *the Crimson Lord* — commands the 1,301st Expedition. The Gal Vorbak fight alongside **[[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Aquillon|Aquillon]]** and the Custodians, their daemons stirring ominously. Then news arrives: **Horus has turned against the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Emperor|Emperor]]**.

The Word Bearers join the Iron Warriors, Night Lords, and Alpha Legion at **[[Heresy_Omnibus/Places/Isstvan V|Isstvan V]]**. As the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Events/Drop Site Massacre|Drop Site Massacre]] unfolds, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Argel Tal|Argel Tal]]’s daemon **[[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Raum|Raum]]** fully awakens, whispering prophecies of their fate. When the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Items/Raven Guard|Raven Guard]] retreat toward the Word Bearers’ lines, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Argel Tal|Argel Tal]] silently asks his long‑dead family for forgiveness before giving the order to fire.

The Gal Vorbak unleash their daemonic forms, becoming monstrous avatars of slaughter. **Corax** kills half their number before duelling Lorgar in a cataclysmic clash. [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Argel Tal|Argel Tal]]’s full daemonic potential erupts, but Lorgar is nearly slain until **[[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Konrad Curze|Konrad Curze]]** intervenes. [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Dagotal|Dagotal]] dies in flames, his death felt by [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Argel Tal|Argel Tal]] like a wound.

After the massacre, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Aquillon|Aquillon]] and his Custodians arrive aboard the *De Profundis* and uncover the Word Bearers’ treachery. They confront Cyrene, killing Incarnadine and then murdering her moments before [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Argel Tal|Argel Tal]] arrives. She dies in his arms.

Consumed by grief and rage, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Argel Tal|Argel Tal]] hunts the Custodians to the surface of [[Heresy_Omnibus/Places/Isstvan V|Isstvan V]]. In the final confrontation, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Malnor|Malnor]] is butchered, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Aquillon|Aquillon]] is decapitated by [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Argel Tal|Argel Tal]], and the last Custodian kills **[[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Xaphen|Xaphen]]** before being torn apart by the surviving Gal Vorbak.

Alone with Cyrene’s final message, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Argel Tal|Argel Tal]] mourns his fallen brothers as the Word Bearers set course for **Calth** to ambush the Ultramarines. In the silence of his mind, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Raum|Raum]] speaks again — declaring that he is [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Argel Tal|Argel Tal]]’s brother now.

## 🧠 Themes
- Faith supplanting duty  
- The birth of heresy through ritual  
- Betrayal as spiritual revelation  

## ⚔️ Legion Focus
- Word Bearers: [[Lorgar Aurelian]], [[Erebus]], [[Kor Phaeron]]  

## 📝 Notes
- The First Heretic reframes loyalty as worship, showing how zealotry can outstrip even genetics.  
- Dark Apostles serve as the ideological backbone for the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Events/Drop Site Massacre|Drop Site Massacre]].  
- [[Heresy_Omnibus/Places/Cadia|Cadia]]’s early exposure to Chaos foreshadows its later role as the Imperium’s bulwark.

## 🔗 Connections
- Builds on the censure event on [[Heresy_Omnibus/Places/Monarchia|Monarchia]] from earlier volumes.  
- Foreshadows the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Events/Drop Site Massacre|Drop Site Massacre]] at [[Heresy_Omnibus/Places/Isstvan V|Isstvan V]].  
- Sets the stage for *[[Heresy_Omnibus/Books/Novels/Galaxy in Flames|Galaxy in Flames]]* and the wider Horus Heresy series.
