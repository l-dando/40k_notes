## 🧭 Overview
*The First Heretic* chronicles the fall of the Word Bearers — the first Legion to turn from the Emperor — and follows their Primarch, [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Lorgar Aurelian|Lorgar]], and Captain [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Argel Tal|Argel Tal]] through the events that ignite the Horus Heresy. It is a story of faith, betrayal, revelation, and damnation, tracing the XVII Legion’s transformation from devout Imperial missionaries into the Imperium’s most fanatical heretics.

---

## Part One — Grey
In 962.M30, the perfect city of **Monarchia**, built by the Word Bearers in worship of the Emperor, is forcibly evacuated and annihilated by the Ultramarines. Among the survivors is **Cyrene Valantion**, blinded by the bombardment. When the Word Bearers arrive, Lorgar confronts **Malcador the Sigillite**, only for the Emperor Himself to descend and condemn Lorgar for worshipping Him as a god. Before the entire Legion, the Emperor forces them to kneel in the dust.

Shattered by this humiliation, Lorgar turns to **Kor Phaeron** and **Erebus**, who tell him the Emperor is a flawed god — and that perhaps the old faith of Colchis held truths he abandoned too quickly. Desperate for meaning, Lorgar agrees to undertake the **Pilgrimage**, an ancient quest to seek the realm where gods and mortals meet.

Returning to Colchis, Lorgar speaks with **Magnus the Red**, who refuses to answer his questions about gods in the Warp. Lorgar appoints Cyrene as a **Confessor** of the Legion and joins the **1,301st Expedition Fleet**, commanded by Argel Tal’s Serrated Sun Chapter, to begin the Pilgrimage beyond Imperial space.

---

## Part Two — Pilgrimage
Three years later, the expedition reaches a region where the Warp bleeds into reality. A psychic scream calls Lorgar’s name from a world on the storm’s edge — **Cadia**. There, the Word Bearers encounter tribes who speak Colchisian, wear human‑skin pelts, and worship gods of the Warp. Their leader, **Ingethel**, welcomes them as prophesied visitors.

During a ritual, Lorgar is told he must choose a tenth sacrifice. When the Custodian **Vendatha** intervenes, violence erupts. Lorgar orders Vendatha impaled, completing the ritual. Ingethel transforms into a daemon — **Ingethel the Ascended** — and offers to reveal the truth of the universe, but demands a price: some of Lorgar’s sons must be offered first. Lorgar chooses **Argel Tal and the Seventh Assault Company**.

Aboard the *Orfeo’s Lament*, Ingethel shows the company visions: the Fall of the Eldar, the Emperor’s secret primarch project beneath Terra, and the revelation that the Emperor bargained with the gods of the Warp to create His sons. Betrayed to their core, the Word Bearers turn against the Emperor in their hearts.

Ingethel then demands they lower the Geller field. When they obey, she slaughters them — only for their corpses to rise again, possessed by daemons. The Seventh Company endures a seven‑month nightmare of cannibalism, blood‑drinking, and madness before escaping the storm. For the rest of the fleet, only seconds have passed.

Lorgar senses the daemons within them and records Argel Tal’s testimony. He embraces the Primordial Truth, exterminates the Cadian tribes to hide their tracks, and elevates the survivors as the **Gal Vorbak**, the first possessed Astartes. In secret, he orders them to hide their nature until the time comes to rebel.

---

## Part Three — Crimson
Forty years later, Argel Tal — now *the Crimson Lord* — commands the 1,301st Expedition. The Gal Vorbak fight alongside **Aquillon** and the Custodians, their daemons stirring ominously. Then news arrives: **Horus has turned against the Emperor**.

The Word Bearers join the Iron Warriors, Night Lords, and Alpha Legion at **Isstvan V**. As the Drop Site Massacre unfolds, Argel Tal’s daemon **Raum** fully awakens, whispering prophecies of their fate. When the Raven Guard retreat toward the Word Bearers’ lines, Argel Tal silently asks his long‑dead family for forgiveness before giving the order to fire.

The Gal Vorbak unleash their daemonic forms, becoming monstrous avatars of slaughter. **Corax** kills half their number before duelling Lorgar in a cataclysmic clash. Argel Tal’s full daemonic potential erupts, but Lorgar is nearly slain until **Konrad Curze** intervenes. Dagotal dies in flames, his death felt by Argel Tal like a wound.

After the massacre, Aquillon and his Custodians arrive aboard the *De Profundis* and uncover the Word Bearers’ treachery. They confront Cyrene, killing Incarnadine and then murdering her moments before Argel Tal arrives. She dies in his arms.

Consumed by grief and rage, Argel Tal hunts the Custodians to the surface of Isstvan V. In the final confrontation, Malnor is butchered, Aquillon is decapitated by Argel Tal, and the last Custodian kills **Xaphen** before being torn apart by the surviving Gal Vorbak.

Alone with Cyrene’s final message, Argel Tal mourns his fallen brothers as the Word Bearers set course for **Calth** to ambush the Ultramarines. In the silence of his mind, Raum speaks again — declaring that he is Argel Tal’s brother now.

## 🧠 Themes

- The birth of heresy through ritual

## 📝 Notes
- The First Heretic reframes loyalty as worship, showing how zealotry can outstrip even genetics.  
- Dark Apostles serve as the ideological backbone for the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Events/Drop Site Massacre|Drop Site Massacre]].  
- [[Heresy_Omnibus/Places/Cadia|Cadia]]’s early exposure to Chaos foreshadows its later role as the Imperium’s bulwark.

## 🔗 Connections
- Builds on the censure event on [[Heresy_Omnibus/Places/Monarchia|Monarchia]] from earlier volumes.  
- Foreshadows the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Events/Drop Site Massacre|Drop Site Massacre]] at [[Heresy_Omnibus/Places/Isstvan V|Isstvan V]].  
- Sets the stage for *Galaxy in Flames* and the wider Horus Heresy series.
