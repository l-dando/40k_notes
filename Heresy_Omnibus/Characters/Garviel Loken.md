## 🧠 Summary
An honourable and principled captain, Loken is respected for his humanity and moral clarity. Chosen for the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Items/Mournival|Mournival]] to temper its more aggressive voices, he becomes a conscience within the Legion.

## ⚔️ Role in the Great Crusade
- Serves with distinction on Sixty‑Three Nineteen, Murder, and during first contact with the Interex.
- Investigates the Whisperheads incident, confronting the reality of Warp entities.
- On [[Heresy_Omnibus/Places/Davin|Davin]], suspects [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Erebus]]’s role in Horus’s wounding after finding the [[Heresy_Omnibus/Items/Anathame|Anathame]].
- Opposes the decision to take Horus to the Serpent Lodge, splitting from [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Ezekyle Abaddon]] and [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Horus Aximand]].
- Works with [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Kyril Sindermann]] and [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Euphrati Keeler]] to uncover Chaos cult links.
- Vows with [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Tarik Torgaddon]] to resist the Legion’s corruption.
- Survives the virus‑bombing on [[Heresy_Omnibus/Places/Isstvan III|Isstvan III]] thanks to [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Saul Tarvitz]]’s warning.
- Fights in the Choral City alongside loyalists from all four Legions.
- Duels [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Khârn]] until a Sons of Horus tank crashes into them, impaling [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Khârn|Khârn]] and ending the fight.
- Confronts [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Ezekyle Abaddon]] in the ruins of the parliament building; grievously wounded and buried in rubble, presumed dead.

## 🔗 Connections
- Trusted by [[Heresy_Omnibus/Characters/Rogal Dorn]].
- Close friendship with Torgaddon; ideological conflict with Abaddon and Aximand.
